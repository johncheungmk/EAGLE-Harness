EAGLE Harness
Architecture Experiment Prototype Paper Guide

Paper and Submission Materials

Suggested title: EAGLE Harness: An Evidence-Aware Governed Lifecycle Engine for Trustworthy Knowledge-Intensive LLM Agents

Recommended article type

Perspective / Review Article with prototype evaluation. The article should not claim empirical improvement until the prototype experiment has been run and the results are added.

Suggested contribution statement

  1. Identify limitations of current agent harnesses for institutional knowledge work.
  2. Propose EAGLE as a governed runtime architecture for knowledge-intensive LLM agents.
  3. Introduce an evidence graph for claim-level traceability and auditability.
  4. Integrate source authority ranking, conflict detection, policy decisions, and risk-aware action control.
  5. Provide a prototype protocol for evaluating EAGLE against standard RAG and agentic RAG.

Pre-submission checklist

  • Run the prototype using 40–70 institutional documents.
  • Prepare 100–150 test questions across factual, conflict, restricted, and action categories.
  • Compare standard RAG, agentic RAG, and EAGLE.
  • Collect 2–3 expert ratings per answer.
  • Add result tables and error analysis to the manuscript.
  • Remove confidential data before publication.

Citation note

The paper should use in-text citations throughout the introduction, related work, framework comparison, evaluation protocol, and discussion. Do not rely only on a reference list at the end.
